The 30-second version

One card. Every connection, remembered.

Subtex is a digital business card that lives on your phone. Instead of handing over paper you'll never see again, you show a QR code — the other person scans it, and both of your cards are exchanged at once. Everyone you meet lands in a searchable collection, so the conversation you had at a conference last spring is still one tap away.

What you'll need Subtex is free on both iPhone and Android. On iPhone you'll need iOS 18.6 or later. Grab it from the App Store or Google Play and you're ready to build your card.

1Create your account

Getting in takes one tap.

Open the app and pick one of three ways in:

  • "Sign in with Apple"
  • "Sign in with Google"
  • "Continue with Email"

If you choose email, the sheet opens on "Create Account". Your password needs at least 8 characters, one uppercase letter, and one number. Coming back later? Switch the toggle to "Sign In" — and if a password slips your mind, the "Forgot password?" link emails you a reset.

2Build your card

Four steps to the card everyone scans.

First launch walks you through a short wizard. This becomes your default card — the one your QR code points at. Here's what each step asks for:

1. Welcome

Nothing to fill in — just tap "Continue" to begin.

2. "Basic Information"

Full name and Title are required. Company, phone, and location are optional — add what's useful, skip what isn't.

3. "Connect Your LinkedIn"

Optional, but worth it. The URL must start with https://www.linkedin.com/in/

4. "Profile Picture"

Tap "Choose Photo" and pick a headshot. Then tap "Complete" to finish.

A photo is required — have one ready You can't finish setup without a profile picture. The "Continue" button stays disabled on the last step until you pick one, and there's no skip. Line up a headshot before you start so nothing stalls you.

You'll also see a one-time prompt titled "Make Subtex Yours" asking about personalization. Answer however you like — it doesn't lock or unlock any features either way.

3Find your way around

Five tabs, and one hidden door.

Everything lives across five tabs at the bottom of the screen:

TabWhat's there
HomePeople you recently met, plus your current and upcoming events
CardsEveryone you've collected — searchable and filterable
EventsEvents you've joined: Current, Upcoming, and Past
TeamsTeams you belong to
ShareYour QR code and the scanner

On iOS 18 and later, Share sits slightly apart from the others as its own round button on the right — that's your fast path to swapping a card.

Where your settings live Not in a tab. Tap your photo in the top-right of Home to open your card and reach Edit Profile, your subscription, app icons, and support.
4Swap cards with someone

The three-second handshake.

  1. Open the Share tab — your QR code is already on screen.
  2. The other person taps "Scan QR Code" and points their camera at it.
  3. Done. Both cards are exchanged at once — they get yours, you get theirs.

You'll feel a haptic tap and see a green checkmark the moment it lands.

Meeting at an event?

A banner appears above your QR reading "Checking in at {event name}", and the connection is filed under that event automatically. If more than one of your events is live, you'll pick from a menu — Subtex won't guess. There's a "Not at an event" option too.

Not in the same room? Share a link

Tap the "Share" button next to the scanner to send a connect link through Messages, Mail, or anything else — perfect for people who aren't standing in front of you. It arrives titled "Add me on Subtex", and tapping it opens a confirmation reading "Connecting adds {name}'s card to your collection and shares your card with them". Nothing happens until they tap "Connect", and links expire after 7 days.

5Work your collection

Everyone you've met, never lost.

Every person you exchange with lives in the Cards tab. This is where a stack of forgotten business cards becomes a network you can actually use.

  • Search matches names, titles, and tags — look for the "Search cards, tags..." field.
  • Filter by "All Cards", "Recently Added", "Sort A-Z", or "Sort Z-A".
  • Tap any card to flip it. The back holds their LinkedIn, email, and phone, plus when you first connected and which events you've shared.
  • Save someone to your phone contacts by flipping the card and tapping "Contact" — it opens your phone's Add Contact screen with everything prefilled.
Shortcut: press and hold You don't even have to open a card to save it. Long-press any card in your collection and you'll get the option to add them straight to your phone contacts.

Notes & Tags Subtex One

Tap a card, then "Notes" to jot private reminders about how you met. "Tags" lets you label the relationship with a primary tag, plus any number of secondary tags.

Primary tags

👔 Colleague 💼 Client 💰 Investor 🤝 Collaborator 🎓 Advisor / Mentor 🧾 Partner / Vendor 😊 Friend ⭐ Other

Secondary tags (add as many as you like)

👑 Decision Maker 🎟️ Met at Event 🛒 Sales Opportunity

Tags follow the person everywhere. Tag someone once and it shows up on their event and team cards too — you'll see "Tags synced across all cards" confirm it.

Ask about a contact Subtex One

The sparkles button next to search opens AI search. Ask things like "How did I meet Art Vandelay?" and it answers from your own collection. You can also generate an "AI Summary" on the back of any card for quick context right before a meeting.

6Add people who aren't on Subtex

Capture the ones who don't have the app.

Met someone without Subtex? Go to Cards → + → "Personal Card". You'll need their name, email, and phone — all three are required. Company, title, and notes are optional.

It gets better if they join later If that person later joins Subtex, you'll get a banner — "{name} just joined Subtex!" — offering to link your offline card to their real profile. Accept, and your notes and tags carry over. Your history stays intact.
7Events

Turn a conference into a connected room.

To join an event: go to Events → + → "Join Event", then enter the code from your organizer. Some events also ask for a password.

Check your inbox When you're added to an event, you'll also receive an email invitation — a handy backup if you ever misplace the join code.

Inside an event you can tap "Check In to Event" (it flips to "Checked In"), read the "Event Brief" if the organizer wrote one, browse the "Attendees" list, and open the event playlist.

Meeting is what unlocks people Attendee profiles stay locked until you've actually scanned each other — you'll see "Scan their QR code to unlock this profile". Contact details, notes, and AI summaries stay hidden until then. That's deliberate: a real handshake is the key.

You can filter attendees by "All", "Connected Here", "Met Before", or "Never Met Before" to see exactly who's still worth meeting. There's no RSVP in Subtex — you join, and you check in.

Event cards are an enterprise feature Event cards, attendee profiles, and in-event scanning are available for enterprise events. At a standard event you'll see a panel reading "Event cards, attendee profiles, notes, and tags are available for enterprise events."
8Teams

How companies stay connected.

Teams are how a company or organization keeps its people connected. You can't create a team in the app — teams are set up by an admin in the Subtex dashboard. Until you're added, a new user sees "You'll see your teams here once an admin adds you to one."

If you have a code, Teams → + → "Join Team" gets you in. Inside, you'll find the team's events, the "Team Hub" brief, the member list, and a "Create Team Card" button for a team-specific version of your card.

10Managing your account

Everything lives behind your card.

Open your card on Home to manage your account. From there you can reach:

  • "Edit Profile" — name, title, company, location, email, phone, LinkedIn, and a bio
  • "App Icon" — six looks Subtex One
  • "Change Email" / "Change Password" — for email/password accounts
  • "Link invite email" — invited under a different address? Enter it here to claim event and team memberships
  • "Help & Support", "Feedback", "Privacy Policy", and "About Subtex"
  • "Sign Out" and "Delete Account"
Deleting your account is permanent It wipes your profile, cards, and connection history, and it cannot be undone. If it's been a while since you signed in, you'll be asked to confirm your identity first.
Quick Troubleshooting

Stuck? Start here.

You need a profile photo. The last step of the wizard won't let you continue without one — pick a headshot and the "Continue" button lights up.

You can't scan your own card. Make sure you're scanning the other person's screen, not your own QR code.

Free accounts stop at 25 collected cards. Upgrade to Subtex One for an unlimited collection.

Codes are issued by the organizer and can expire. Ask them to resend the current one.

You were probably invited under a different email. Use "Link invite email" in your Profile to claim your event and team memberships.

Teams are admin-assigned. Ask your admin to add you, or get a join code from them and use Teams → + → "Join Team".
